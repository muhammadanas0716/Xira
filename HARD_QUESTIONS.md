# Hard Questions for Financial Analysis Testing

This document contains challenging questions to test the AI's ability to analyze SEC 10-Q filings and provide deep financial insights.

## Financial Performance & Analysis

1. **What is the company's revenue recognition policy, and how does it impact the reported revenue for this quarter?**

2. **Calculate the company's free cash flow yield and compare it to the previous quarter. What does this tell us about the company's financial health?**

3. **What are the key drivers behind the change in operating margin between this quarter and the same quarter last year? Break down each contributing factor.**

4. **Analyze the company's earnings quality by examining the relationship between cash flow from operations and net income. Are there any red flags?**

5. **What percentage of revenue growth is organic versus from acquisitions? How sustainable is the current growth rate?**

6. **Compare the company's gross margin trends across the last 4 quarters. What factors are driving the changes?**

7. **What is the company's return on invested capital (ROIC) for this quarter, and how does it compare to their weighted average cost of capital (WACC)?**

8. **Identify any one-time items, restructuring charges, or non-recurring expenses that affected this quarter's earnings. What would normalized earnings look like?**

## Balance Sheet & Capital Structure

9. **What is the company's debt-to-equity ratio, and how has it changed over the past year? What are the implications for financial risk?**

10. **Analyze the company's working capital management. Is there evidence of aggressive working capital optimization that could signal future problems?**

11. **What is the breakdown of the company's debt by maturity? Are there any upcoming debt maturities that could create liquidity pressure?**

12. **Calculate the company's current ratio and quick ratio. How do these compare to industry standards, and what do they indicate about short-term liquidity?**

13. **What off-balance sheet liabilities or commitments does the company have? How significant are they relative to reported liabilities?**

14. **Examine the company's goodwill and intangible assets. Are there any impairment risks or concerns about overvaluation?**

15. **What is the company's tangible book value per share, and how does it compare to the stock price?**

## Cash Flow Analysis

16. **Why did cash flow from operations differ significantly from net income this quarter? Provide a detailed reconciliation.**

17. **What percentage of capital expenditures is maintenance capex versus growth capex? How does this impact free cash flow sustainability?**

18. **Analyze the company's cash conversion cycle. Has it improved or deteriorated, and what are the implications?**

19. **What are the main uses of cash this quarter? Are they aligned with the company's stated strategic priorities?**

20. **Compare operating cash flow margins across the last 4 quarters. What trends are evident, and what do they suggest about business quality?**

## Segment & Geographic Analysis

21. **Which business segment or geographic region showed the strongest performance this quarter, and what were the key drivers?**

22. **If the company operates multiple segments, which segment has the highest operating margin, and why?**

23. **What percentage of revenue comes from the company's top 3 customers or geographic regions? What are the concentration risks?**

24. **How has the company's geographic revenue mix changed over the past year? What are the implications for currency exposure?**

## Risk Assessment

25. **What are the top 3 financial risks identified in the risk factors section, and how are they being mitigated?**

26. **What is the company's exposure to interest rate risk? How would a 200 basis point increase in rates affect their financials?**

27. **Are there any contingent liabilities or legal proceedings that could materially impact the company's financial position?**

28. **What is the company's pension or post-retirement benefit obligation, and how well-funded is it?**

29. **What are the key assumptions in the company's critical accounting estimates, and how sensitive are the financials to changes in these assumptions?**

## Competitive & Strategic Analysis

30. **What is the company's market share trend in their primary business segments, and what competitive dynamics are affecting it?**

31. **How does the company's R&D spending as a percentage of revenue compare to industry peers? Is it sufficient to maintain competitiveness?**

32. **What strategic initiatives or investments were highlighted this quarter? How are they being funded, and what is the expected timeline for returns?**

33. **What is the company's customer acquisition cost and lifetime value? How has customer retention changed?**

## Forward-Looking & Guidance

34. **What forward-looking statements or guidance did management provide? What are the key assumptions underlying their outlook?**

35. **What are management's expectations for the next quarter, and how do they compare to analyst consensus?**

36. **What headwinds or tailwinds does management expect in the coming quarters, and how are they preparing?**

37. **What is the company's capital allocation strategy? How are they balancing dividends, buybacks, debt reduction, and growth investments?**

## Accounting & Technical Analysis

38. **What accounting method changes or new standards were adopted this quarter? How did they impact the financial statements?**

39. **Are there any significant differences between GAAP and non-GAAP earnings? What adjustments were made, and are they justified?**

40. **What is the company's effective tax rate, and how does it compare to the statutory rate? What are the main drivers of the difference?**

41. **Examine the footnotes for any related-party transactions. Are there any concerns about conflicts of interest?**

42. **What is the company's inventory valuation method, and are there any LIFO/FIFO impacts or inventory obsolescence risks?**

## Valuation & Investment Analysis

43. **Based on the financial data, what is a reasonable price-to-earnings multiple for this company, and how does the current valuation compare?**

44. **What is the company's enterprise value to EBITDA ratio, and how does it compare to comparable companies?**

45. **Calculate the company's economic value added (EVA) for this quarter. Is the company creating or destroying shareholder value?**

46. **What is the company's dividend payout ratio and dividend coverage ratio? Is the dividend sustainable?**

47. **If you were to value this company using a discounted cash flow model, what would be your key assumptions based on the filing data?**

## Complex Multi-Part Questions

48. **Perform a comprehensive DuPont analysis breaking down return on equity into its components (profit margin, asset turnover, equity multiplier). How has each component changed, and what does this tell us about the company's strategy?**

49. **Compare this quarter's performance to the same quarter last year across revenue, margins, cash flow, and balance sheet metrics. Create a comprehensive analysis identifying the 5 most significant changes and their implications.**

50. **Analyze the company's capital structure optimization. Given their current debt levels, interest rates, tax situation, and cash position, is their capital structure optimal? What would you recommend?**

51. **Examine the relationship between revenue growth, margin expansion/contraction, and cash generation. Is the company's growth profitable and cash-generative, or are they sacrificing profitability for growth?**

52. **What are the key metrics that would indicate this company is in financial distress? Evaluate the company against these metrics and provide an assessment.**

53. **If you were a credit analyst, what would be your credit rating recommendation for this company? Justify your answer with specific financial metrics and ratios from the filing.**

54. **Compare the company's financial performance to their stated strategic objectives. Are they on track, and what gaps exist between performance and goals?**

55. **What would need to happen for this company to become a value trap versus a value opportunity? Identify the key financial indicators to watch.**

## Stress Testing & Scenario Analysis

56. **If revenue declined by 20% next quarter, what would be the impact on cash flow, debt covenants, and the company's ability to service its obligations?**

57. **What is the company's break-even revenue level, and how much cushion do they have above that level?**

58. **How would a recession scenario (30% revenue decline, margin compression) affect the company's balance sheet and liquidity over the next 12 months?**

59. **What is the company's maximum debt capacity based on their current cash flow generation and debt covenants?**

## Industry-Specific Deep Dives

60. **For tech companies: What is the company's customer lifetime value to customer acquisition cost ratio? How does their unit economics look?**

61. **For manufacturing companies: What is the company's capacity utilization rate, and are there any capacity constraints or excess capacity issues?**

62. **For retail companies: What is the company's same-store sales growth, and how does it compare to new store expansion?**

63. **For financial companies: What is the company's loan loss reserve adequacy, and how does it compare to actual charge-offs?**

64. **For healthcare/pharma companies: What is the company's R&D pipeline value, and how does R&D spending compare to potential future revenue?**

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

65. **Are there any accounting red flags in this filing? Look for aggressive revenue recognition, unusual transactions, or changes in accounting methods.**

66. **What are the warning signs that this company might be experiencing financial distress or heading toward bankruptcy?**

67. **Are there any discrepancies between the cash flow statement, income statement, and balance sheet that raise concerns?**

68. **What management compensation metrics are tied to financial performance? Are there any perverse incentives that could lead to short-termism?**

## Comparative Analysis

69. **How does this company's financial performance compare to the best-in-class company in their industry? What are the key gaps?**

70. **What would this company's financials look like if they had the same operating margin as their top competitor?**

71. **If this company merged with their closest competitor, what would the combined financial profile look like? What are the synergies and risks?**

## Advanced Financial Modeling Questions

72. **Build a simple 3-statement model projection for the next 4 quarters based on trends in this filing. What are your key assumptions?**

73. **What is the company's sustainable growth rate based on their return on equity and retention ratio?**

74. **Calculate the company's Altman Z-score. What does it indicate about bankruptcy risk?**

75. **What is the company's Piotroski F-score based on this quarter's financials? What does it suggest about financial health?**

## Usage Tips

- Start with simpler questions to establish context, then move to more complex multi-part questions
- Use questions that require calculations to test the AI's ability to extract and process numerical data
- Questions requiring comparisons across periods test the AI's ability to track changes over time
- Multi-part questions test the AI's ability to synthesize information from multiple sections
- Questions about footnotes and accounting policies test deep document understanding
- Forward-looking questions test the AI's ability to infer implications from current data

## Testing Strategy

1. **Basic Extraction**: Start with questions requiring simple data extraction
2. **Calculations**: Test mathematical capabilities with ratio and metric calculations
3. **Comparisons**: Test ability to compare across periods and segments
4. **Analysis**: Test deeper analytical thinking and interpretation
5. **Synthesis**: Test ability to combine multiple data points into insights
6. **Edge Cases**: Test with questions about missing data or ambiguous situations
