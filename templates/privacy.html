1. Introduction

Xira ("we," "our," "us," or "the Company") operates a financial research and analysis platform (the "Service" or "Platform"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information.

This Privacy Policy describes our practices regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of information when you use our Service. By accessing or using Xira, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our Service.

This policy applies to all users of our Service, including visitors, registered users, and anyone who accesses our Platform through any means, including web browsers, mobile devices, or APIs.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when using our Service, including but not limited to:

  • Stock Ticker Symbols: Ticker symbols you search for, analyze, or request information about
  • Queries and Questions: Questions, queries, and analysis requests you submit about financial documents and securities
  • Chat Conversations: Complete chat history, including questions asked and responses received
  • Analysis Requests: Requests for financial reports, summaries, and analytical insights
  • Account Information: If you create an account, we may collect name, email address, and other registration information
  • Communication Data: Correspondence with our support team, feedback, and survey responses

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you access or use our Service, we automatically collect certain technical information, including:

  • Device Information: IP address, device type, operating system, browser type and version, device identifiers, and mobile network information
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features used, time spent on pages, clickstream data, search queries, and navigation patterns
  • Access Logs: Timestamps of access, referring URLs, exit pages, and session duration
  • Performance Data: Response times, error messages, and system performance metrics
  • Location Data: General geographic location derived from IP address (country and city level, not precise location)

2.3 Financial and Market Data

In the course of providing our Service, we collect and process financial information related to securities and companies you analyze:

  • Stock Market Data: Real-time and historical stock prices, trading volumes, market capitalization, and financial metrics
  • SEC Filings: Downloaded SEC filing documents (10-Q quarterly reports, 10-K annual reports, and other regulatory filings)
  • Company Information: Company names, ticker symbols, sector classifications, and business descriptions
  • Financial Metrics: P/E ratios, revenue, earnings, debt levels, and other financial indicators
  • Analysis Results: AI-generated reports, summaries, and insights based on financial documents

  • Provide customer support and technical assistance
  • Send administrative information, such as changes to our terms or policies

3.4 Legal and Security

  • Comply with legal obligations, court orders, and regulatory requirements

4. Third-Party Services and Data Sharing

4.1 Third-Party Service Providers

Our Service integrates with third-party services to provide functionality. When you use our Service, your information may be shared with:

  • Stock Data Providers (yfinance, Polygon.io): We share ticker symbols and requests to fetch real-time stock market data, prices, financial metrics, and company information. These providers have their own privacy policies governing data use.
  • SEC API Services: We share ticker symbols and filing requests to access and download SEC filing documents. SEC filings are public records, but API providers may log requests.
  • AI Service Providers (OpenAI): We share your questions, queries, and financial document content with AI services to generate analysis, summaries, and answers. AI providers may use this data to improve their models, subject to their privacy policies.
  • Cloud Infrastructure Providers: We use cloud hosting services to store and process data. These providers have access to data as necessary to provide hosting services.
  • Analytics Services: We may use analytics tools to understand usage patterns and improve our Service.

Important: When we share your information with third-party service providers, they are contractually obligated to use your information only for the purposes we specify and in accordance with this Privacy Policy. However, third-party services have their own privacy policies, and we encourage you to review them.

AI Model Training: User data, including questions, queries, and financial document content shared with AI service providers (such as OpenAI), is NOT used to train or improve public AI models unless you explicitly opt-in to such use. By default, your data is used solely to generate responses to your queries and is not used for model training purposes.

4.2 Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes. We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers: With third-party vendors, consultants, and service providers who perform services on our behalf (e.g., hosting, analytics, customer support)

5. Data Storage, Retention, and Security

5.1 Data Storage

Your information is stored on secure servers located in the United States and may be processed in other countries where our service providers operate. By using our Service, you consent to the transfer of your information to these locations.

We store the following types of data:

  • Chat Conversations: Stored on our servers to maintain conversation context
  • SEC Filing Documents: Downloaded PDFs are stored locally on our servers for processing and analysis
  • Analysis Reports: Generated reports and summaries are stored to enable retrieval and review
  • Usage Data: Logs and analytics data are retained for service improvement and troubleshooting

5.2 Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide our Service and fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy
  • Comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements
  • Maintain security and prevent fraud or abuse

Retention Periods:

  • Chat conversations and analysis data: Retained for 12 months from last activity, unless you request deletion
  • SEC filing documents: Retained for 90 days after last access, then deleted
  • Usage logs and analytics: Retained for 24 months, then anonymized or deleted
  • Account information: Retained until account deletion or 3 years of inactivity

You may request deletion of your data at any time by contacting us at anas@bridged.vu. We will delete your data within 30 days of your request, subject to legal retention requirements.

5.3 Security Measures

We implement industry-standard technical and organizational security measures to protect your information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction:

  • Encryption: Data in transit is encrypted using TLS/SSL protocols. Sensitive data at rest is encrypted using industry-standard encryption algorithms
  • Access Controls: Strict access controls and authentication mechanisms limit access to your information to authorized personnel only
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular security audits protect our infrastructure
  • Regular Updates: We keep our systems and software updated with the latest security patches
  • Security Monitoring: Continuous monitoring for security threats, vulnerabilities, and suspicious activity
  • Employee Training: Our team receives regular security training and is bound by confidentiality obligations

Security Disclaimer: While we implement robust security measures, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information. You use our Service at your own risk.

5.4 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that compromises your personal information, we will notify you and relevant authorities as required by applicable law:

  • GDPR Compliance: We will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, and notify affected users without undue delay if the breach poses a high risk to their rights and freedoms
  • General Notification: We will notify affected users via email or through a prominent notice on our Service as soon as reasonably possible, typically within 72 hours of discovery
  • Notification Content: Our breach notification will include information about the nature of the breach, the types of information affected, steps we are taking to address the breach, and recommendations for protecting yourself

We maintain incident response procedures and will take immediate steps to contain, investigate, and remediate any security breach.

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to collect and store information about your use of our Service.

7.1 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the Service to function properly (e.g., session management, security). These cookies are necessary and cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how users interact with our Service (e.g., page views, feature usage). These are non-essential and require your consent.
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences. These are non-essential and require your consent.
  • Performance Cookies: Collect information about Service performance and errors. These are non-essential and require your consent.

7.2 Cookie Consent

In compliance with GDPR and other applicable privacy laws, we will request your explicit consent before placing non-essential cookies on your device. You will be presented with a cookie consent banner when you first visit our Service, allowing you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your cookie preferences at any time through your account settings or by contacting us.

Essential cookies are necessary for the Service to function and do not require consent. However, you can still control these through your browser settings, though this may impact Service functionality.

7.3 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies. However, if you disable cookies, some features of our Service may not function properly. For more information about managing cookies, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.
